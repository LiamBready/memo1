# The Burnout Badger

## Persona
Developer dealing with burnout -- they used to love coding, now the IDE feels like a threat, and every "quick task" feels like climbing Everest in flip-flops.

## The Problem They Have
They cannot start because nothing feels worth starting. The backlog is full, but nothing on it sparks anything. They need wins smaller than they have ever allowed themselves.

## The NanoClaw Setup
- Sends micro-challenges so small they genuinely cannot be declined ("rename one variable to something you actually like")
- Fires unprompted dev comics and git-history callbacks to rebuild the emotional connection with the work
- Spawns a throwaway fun repo when the pattern persists -- no stakes, no PRs, just something playful
- Quietly celebrates streaks of three consecutive tiny commits

## Sample Telegram Interaction
> **Agent:** One thing today: open auth.py and rename the temp variable to something better. That is it.
> **Dev:** That feels stupid.
> **Agent:** Yes. Do it anyway.
> **Dev:** Done. Called it session_token.
> **Agent:** You opened the IDE. That was the hard part. See you tomorrow.

## Why It's a Digital Twin
It meets you where burnout left you and walks at whatever pace you can manage.
