# The CTO Research Radar

## Persona
Startup CTO juggling product vision, fundraising, and a growing AI stack who barely has time to read the latest LLM papers.

## The Problem They Have
Important breakthroughs slip by unnoticed, leading to missed opportunities and awkward "I didn't know that existed" moments in investor meetings.

## The NanoClaw Setup
- Subscribes to arXiv AI-section RSS and filters for papers matching their interest tags (agents, RAG, AI safety)
- For each relevant paper, generates a 3-bullet TL;DR + "why it matters for our stack" and posts to a private Telegram channel
- If unread for 48h, sends a nudge: "2-minute read. No jargon. Relevant to what you're building."
- Weekly, compiles the top 3 papers into an audio-friendly summary for commute listening

## Sample Telegram Interaction
> **Agent:** 📄 "Self-Reflective Agents for Dynamic Tool Use" — agents learn to ask for help when stuck → fewer failed API calls.
> **CTO:** That's exactly what we're hitting.
> **Agent:** Want a prototype branch with a reflection wrapper around our MCP calls?
> **CTO:** Yes.
> **Agent:** Branch `reflex-agent` created. PR ready when you are.

## Why It's a Digital Twin
It acts as your ever-vigilant research assistant — surfacing what matters before the next board meeting.
